What all three comparisons have in common

The pattern across every comparison is the same: tools built for a different job get blamed when leads go cold. The actual problem is almost always an intake layer that was never designed to handle high-consideration service inquiries.

What a purpose-built intake system does

  • Responds to every inbound inquiry within minutes, not hours
  • Qualifies and routes based on service type and urgency
  • Captures after-hours and missed-call demand before it disappears
  • Maintains a follow-up queue so nothing goes cold by default
  • Hands off to a human with context, not a raw inbox thread

Why the alternatives fall short on their own

  • Receptionists are unavailable outside business hours and during high volume
  • CRMs only act on data someone manually entered first
  • Generic chatbots route people away from conversion, not toward it
  • None of them are built to recover leads that went cold 2–5 days after first contact
