Local services

Turn more local service inquiries into booked conversations.

The competitor who answers first wins most local service work. If your intake relies on checking messages between jobs, or following up when you get a chance, you are losing work that was already in your hands.

The three intake failures that cost local operators the most

Local service businesses do not usually lose work at the marketing stage. They lose it in the gap between the inquiry arriving and the conversation being booked.

Intake failure 1

Quote requests that disappear

A homeowner submits a quote request on your website at 7pm. You see it the next morning. You reply at 10am. They already have two quotes and a scheduled site visit from businesses that replied within the hour. High-ticket local work — renovation, landscaping, specialty services — goes to the fastest credible respondent, not the cheapest or the best.

IXIA installs an immediate response layer for form and inquiry submissions — a fast, professional acknowledgement that buys time and keeps you in the conversation while your team prepares a proper reply.

Intake failure 2

No system after the first call

The first call goes well. You say you will send a quote this week. A week passes. They call someone else. Most local service operators have no structured process between initial contact and signed work order — it runs on memory, mental notes, and good intentions. When you are busy, it breaks.

IXIA builds a lightweight lead pipeline — not a full CRM — that tells you what stage each lead is in, what needs to happen next, and when follow-up is overdue.

Intake failure 3

Competitor responds first

Local search and Google Ads send inquiries to multiple providers simultaneously. The window between inquiry and decision is often 24–48 hours. If your response time is measured in days rather than minutes, you are effectively running a marketing budget that feeds your competitors' pipelines.

IXIA designs your first-response logic so that every inquiry — regardless of source, time of day, or channel — gets a credible, fast response that keeps you in contention.

What IXIA builds for local service operators

  • Instant inquiry acknowledgement — automatic, professional response for form and call inquiries within minutes of arrival
  • Quote follow-up sequences — 2–4 touchpoints for prospects who received a quote but went quiet
  • After-hours capture — text or email response for inquiries outside business hours, with a next-morning priority flag
  • Lead stage tracking — simple pipeline view showing which leads need a call, a quote, or a follow-up today
  • Source-specific response scripts — different voice and focus for Google Ads, referral, and organic leads

Who this fits

  • Contractors and trades businesses receiving 10–100 inquiries per month with inconsistent close rates
  • Specialty service providers — landscaping, renovation, cleaning, wellness, education — where one booking is worth $1,000+
  • Owner-operators who are excellent at the work but stretched thin on the business side
  • Multi-location service businesses where intake quality differs by location or by who picked up the phone
