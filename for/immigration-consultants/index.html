Immigration sector

Capture and qualify immigration leads before they go to your competitor.

Immigration inquiries arrive with urgency, complexity, and a short window for engagement. Slow intake, buried eligibility questions, and dropped follow-up are the three places most consultants bleed clients they already paid to attract.

Why immigration intake fails more often than most sectors

Immigration clients are not just evaluating price. They are evaluating competence, trustworthiness, and responsiveness — under significant personal stress. Your intake process is the first signal they get about all three.

Intake failure 1

Complex eligibility questions handled too late

A skilled worker visa inquiry and a family sponsorship inquiry look identical in your inbox — but they require completely different qualification questions, processing timelines, and service tiers. Without structured intake triage, both sit in a queue waiting for a consultant to open them manually.

IXIA builds eligibility triage logic that classifies inquiry type on arrival — pathway, nationality, urgency — and routes it to the right consultant with the relevant context pre-filled.

Intake failure 2

Document collection creates drop-off

You request documents via email. The client sends them one at a time over two weeks, or goes silent after the first request. Each exchange is a manual follow-up that burns consultant time and gives the prospect a reason to wonder if the firm is on top of their case.

IXIA installs a structured document collection flow — staged, auto-reminded, and tracked — so your team always knows what has been received, what is pending, and who is stalled.

Intake failure 3

Long decision cycles kill the pipeline

Immigration is a high-consideration purchase. Many prospects research for weeks or months before committing. Without a sustained follow-up sequence, qualified prospects who said "I need to think about it" disappear from your pipeline and re-appear in a competitor's signed-client list.

IXIA designs long-cycle nurture sequences specific to immigration — calibrated to pathway type, decision horizon, and the information gaps that cause hesitation.

What IXIA builds for immigration practices

  • Pathway triage logic — classifies and routes inquiries by visa type, nationality, and urgency before a consultant touches them
  • Structured eligibility intake — collects the right pre-qualification information per pathway, reducing back-and-forth
  • Document collection workflow — staged requests with automatic reminders and a clear status view for your team
  • Long-cycle follow-up sequences — for prospects in the research or "thinking about it" phase, keeps your firm visible without spamming
  • Consultation booking flow — clean path from qualified lead to paid initial consultation

Who this fits

  • RCIC-regulated consultants or immigration law practices handling 20+ inquiries per month
  • Firms managing multiple pathway types where intake quality varies by case complexity
  • Practices that have tried CRMs but find them too generic for immigration-specific stages
  • Consultants who receive strong referral volume but find it falls into a disorganized inbox process
